\section{Neural Field Methods}
\label{sec:neural_field_methods}

Neural implicit representations — particularly neural radiance fields (NeRF) and their variants — have been adapted for CT reconstruction by representing the 3D attenuation field as a continuous function parameterised by a multi-layer perceptron (MLP).

\subsection{Key Approaches}

\textbf{Neural Attenuation Fields (NAF)} \cite{zha2022naf} reformulates sparse-view cone-beam CT (CBCT) reconstruction as learning a neural field that maps 3D coordinates to attenuation coefficients. The network is optimised by minimising the discrepancy between projections rendered from the learned field and the observed X-ray images, analogous to NeRF-style volumetric rendering.

\subsection{Discussion}

Neural field methods are attractive because they are inherently resolution-agnostic and can be trained per-scene without requiring large paired datasets. However, per-scene optimisation is computationally expensive (often requiring minutes to hours per volume), limiting clinical applicability. Recent work on generalizable neural fields and meta-learning-based initialisation aims to address this limitation.
